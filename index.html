MyHL for Hyperliquid — Privacy Policy

Effective July 6, 2026 · STRIDE LABS INC · riley@stridelabs.co

This policy describes how the MyHL for Hyperliquid Chrome extension (“MyHL”, “we”, “us”) handles information when you use it on app.hyperliquid.xyz.

Scope

MyHL runs only on Hyperliquid’s web app. It does not run on other websites. MyHL is not affiliated with Hyperliquid.

Live data mode (default)

New installs use Live data by default. MyHL contacts our hosted read-only data API at myhl-production.up.railway.app:

What is sentWhenStored by MyHL?
Your public Ethereum wallet (master) address Sub-Accounts per-DEX equity popovers (to look up your sub-account balances) No long-term database. In-memory cache on our server for up to 30 seconds, then discarded.
Public market identifiers (coin symbols) Chart overlay layers you enable (funding, L2 book, diffs, calendar) Public market data only; in-memory cache from 5 seconds to 5 minutes depending on data type, then discarded.

Our server proxies Hyperliquid’s public API to return read-only market data and sub-account equity to your browser. We use this solely to render the overlays and sub-account views you see on the page.

Demo data mode

You can switch to Demo data in the extension brand menu. In Demo mode, MyHL does not contact MyHL servers. Chart overlays, World Cup odds, and sub-account views use bundled or synthetic sample data shipped inside the extension package. Your public wallet address is not sent to us in Demo mode. An in-product disclosure appears if you switch from Demo back to Live.

Direct requests to Hyperliquid

MyHL may send one optional request directly from your browser to api.hyperliquid.xyz for public market metadata (asset leverage limits) used by the what-if liquidation calculator. That request does not include your public wallet address.

Hyperliquid’s own site traffic (including data Hyperliquid loads for your session) is governed by Hyperliquid’s policies, not this policy.

Trading and transfers (signed on your device)

Some optional features let you take actions on Hyperliquid. When you use them, the request is signed on your device and sent directly to Hyperliquid (api.hyperliquid.xyz) — it does not pass through MyHL servers.

  • World Cup orders. Trading is off until you turn it on, which opens Hyperliquid’s own “Enable Trading” flow. MyHL reuses the trading connection Hyperliquid establishes — the agent key that Hyperliquid creates and stores in your browser — to sign the outcome orders you place. MyHL does not create, store, or transmit any key of its own, and the key is never sent to MyHL.
  • Sub-account transfers. Moving USDC between your own Spot and Perps balances is signed by your own external wallet (for example, MetaMask). MyHL never holds or receives your wallet’s private keys or seed phrase.

What MyHL never receives

These never leave your device to MyHL servers:

  • Your wallet private keys or seed phrase.
  • Hyperliquid’s trading key — MyHL reuses the key Hyperliquid stores in your browser to sign on your device; MyHL never creates, stores, or receives it.
  • The orders and transfers you sign — they go directly to Hyperliquid, not through MyHL.
  • Analytics or usage telemetry.

How your public wallet address is used

MyHL reads your public Ethereum wallet address from the Hyperliquid page (URL, UI, or Hyperliquid’s own API traffic on the page) to power features in your browser. In Live data mode (the default), that public address is sent to our API only when you open Sub-Accounts per-DEX equity popovers (to look up your sub-account balances). In Demo data mode, your public wallet address is never sent to MyHL servers.

Local storage

Feature toggles, overlay preferences, and your Demo/Live choice are stored in your browser’s localStorage on your device; they are not synced to MyHL servers. For World Cup orders, MyHL only reads Hyperliquid’s existing trading key from your browser (created by Hyperliquid’s own “Enable Trading” flow) to sign orders you place — MyHL does not write or store that key.

Third parties

  • Hyperliquid — public API used as the data source when Live mode is on.
  • Railway — hosts the MyHL data API. Platform HTTP logs may transiently include request URLs.

Chrome Web Store Limited Use

MyHL’s use of information received from Google APIs or Chrome Web Store user data adheres to the Chrome Web Store User Data Policy, including the Limited Use requirements. We do not sell user data. We do not use public wallet or account data for purposes unrelated to MyHL’s core functionality (World Cup odds/trading, chart overlays, and sub-account transfers on Hyperliquid). We do not use this data to determine creditworthiness or for lending.

Security

Live-mode API traffic uses HTTPS. No executable code is downloaded from our servers; only JSON data is fetched.

Changes

We may update this policy when MyHL’s behavior changes. The effective date at the top will be revised. Continued use after an update constitutes acceptance of the revised policy.
